SAP Procurement FAQ Dataset
===========================

Context: SAP S/4HANA Cloud (Public Edition), Sourcing and Procurement line of
business. SAP S/4HANA Cloud is part of SAP Business Suite and runs on SAP
Business Technology Platform (BTP), with AI capabilities embedded across
procurement processes.

Q1: What is a Purchase Requisition (PR)?
A1: A PR is an internal document used to request the procurement of materials or
services. It captures the requirement (material/service, quantity, delivery date
or service period, plant, account assignment) and triggers approval and sourcing
before a Purchase Order is created. Self-Service Requisitioning and the
professional "My Purchase Requisitions" Fiori apps are used to create and process
PRs.

Q2: When should I create a PR instead of a direct PO?
A2: Create a PR when end users need to request items and a buyer must perform
sourcing, control budgets, or ensure approvals before a formal PO is sent to a
supplier. Intake-management scenarios route all requests through a single entry
point so they are captured, guided, and approved consistently.

Q3: What is a Purchase Order (PO)?
A3: A PO is a legally binding document sent to a supplier that specifies
materials or services, quantities, prices, delivery dates or service periods,
and terms. PO numbers in this dataset follow the 45XXXXXXXX range.

Q4: How is a PO created in the P2P process?
A4: Buyers convert approved PRs or accepted supplier quotations into POs. The
system copies master data such as material, supplier Business Partner, and
pricing conditions, applies the calculation schema for net pricing and taxes, and
can enforce flexible approval workflows and output rules. POs are transmitted via
email, EDI, or SAP Business Network.

Q5: What is Goods Receipt (GR)?
A5: GR records the receipt of goods against a PO. It updates stock and valuation,
records quantities received, posts accounting entries, and supports three-way
matching with the PO and invoice. For services, a Service Entry Sheet or lean
service confirmation is used instead of a physical goods receipt.

Q6: What is Invoice Verification (Supplier Invoice processing)?
A6: Supplier invoices are checked against the PO and (where applicable) the GR
for quantity, price, and tax accuracy before posting to the ledger and creating
open payables. The Supplier Invoice Fiori apps support posting, parking, and the
release of blocked invoices.

Q7: What is the GR/IR account?
A7: The GR/IR (Goods Receipt/Invoice Receipt) clearing account temporarily holds
the difference between received goods and posted invoices. When GR and invoice
quantities and values match within tolerance, the GR/IR balance for that item is
cleared.

Q8: What is the difference between two-way and three-way matching?
A8: Three-way matching compares PO, GR, and invoice for quantity, price, and
terms (typical for materials). Two-way matching compares PO and invoice only
(used where no goods receipt applies). Invoices outside tolerance are blocked for
payment and routed for resolution.

Q9: What is the difference between a contract and a PO?
A9: A contract (quantity or value) defines agreed terms, conditions, and targets
with a supplier over a period; a PO is a specific order that calls off quantities
or values against that contract. Contract Management sits within the Sourcing and
Contract Management area.

Q10: Can I create a PO without a PR?
A10: Yes, buyers can create POs directly in some scenarios (recurring services,
urgent purchases), but organizations typically restrict this with workflows,
thresholds, and policies to maintain control and auditability.

Q11: How are service purchases handled today?
A11: Services are handled via Lean Services, SAP's strategic solution for service
procurement. Service items use a service period (Start Date / End Date) rather
than a single delivery date. After the supplier performs the service, a service
entry sheet or confirmation is created and approved before invoice posting. The
legacy MM-SRV model remains available but no longer receives functional
enhancements.

Q12: What is Central Procurement?
A12: Central Procurement lets a central buying team in one hub system create and
manage requisitions, purchase orders, and contracts on behalf of multiple
connected backend systems, enabling group-level control, harmonized sourcing, and
cross-system analytics.

Q13: How does AI help in SAP procurement?
A13: SAP embeds AI across the process. Joule, SAP's enterprise copilot, provides
natural-language interaction and navigation - for example "show purchase orders"
or "upload a supplier invoice" - and routes users to the right Fiori app under
their existing authorizations. AI-assisted features such as Easy Filter
(natural-language list filtering) and Smart Summarization speed up day-to-day
work, and machine-learning analytics support spend visibility and supplier
insights.

Q14: How do suppliers collaborate electronically?
A14: SAP Business Network supports PO transmission, order confirmations, advance
shipping notifications, and electronic invoicing. Next-generation SAP Ariba adds
Intake Management as a central entry point and Joule-assisted sourcing, rolling
out progressively through 2026 and 2027.

Q15: How are SAP S/4HANA Cloud releases delivered?
A15: SAP S/4HANA Cloud Public Edition receives frequent releases identified by a
YYMM code (for example 2508, 2602). The Private Edition follows an annual release
(for example 2025) with feature pack stacks. New procurement capabilities are
documented in each release's "What's New" notes.
